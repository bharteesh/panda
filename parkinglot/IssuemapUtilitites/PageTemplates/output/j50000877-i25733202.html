2Did cavalry become obsolete after the First World War? Or was it transformed, the better to fit the wars of the new century? Of little impact in the first months of war, cavalry seemed to have become useless as armies proceeded to dig trenches. Units were then disbanded and many troopers were assigned to other services. At the same time, military leaders sought ways to increase the fire-power and defensive capacity of cavalry. Thus was cavalry transformed, with more and more troopers dismounted from their horses to ride instead in tanks. Armoured cavalry was born.
