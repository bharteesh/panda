2Even if Simondon, as Jean-Hugues Barthelemy well noted in Penser la connaissance et la technique apres Simondon (« How one may think knowledge and technique after Simondon » ) , indeed stresses the stabilization part played by the trans-individual granted as it may be to technique, yet the latter never analyzes the intrinsically technological dimension of the pre-individual. That is why religion, which Simondon originates however amid the magical, is no longer thought since its deeply technical constitution. Now such a question goes back to where Simondon moves towards psychoanalysis : the Simondon criticism of the latter never clearly formulated the objection that should be addressed to it, i. e. never taking into thought the part played by technique in individuation, i. e. within desire.

1Qu'est-ce qu'un mouvement réel ? En posant cette question avec une vigueur nouvelle, Simondon a aussi redonné sens à quelques moments essentiels de la philosophic française. Simondon avait lu Maine de Biran et Bergson, et sera lu comme eux par Deleuze. Par-delà ces rencontres factuelles, est passée entre eux une façon d'élever en problème majeur la question du mouvement, et de ne pas séparer mouvement, conscience et tout. 2What is a real movement ? Asking such a question with a true new vigour, Simondon also gave a new meaning to quite a few essential moments of French philosophy. Simondon had indeed read Maine de Biran and Bergson and was to be read by Deleuze. Beyond such factual encounters, a new approach took place between them all, enabling them to turn movement into a major problem and not to separate movement, conscience and whole. It is such a twofold dimension  i. e. both metaphysical and logical  (according to Simondon) of movement that we attempted to recapture.
