2In Sense and Sensibilia, Austin give a critical analysis of the usual conception of perception which states that we do not perceive directly material objects but sense data, ideas, or impressions. He underlines, against Descartes, that our senses are dumb and point out that the aim of the argument of sensory illusion is to posit some sort of intermediary entities. In this paper, I focus on the cartesian example of the stick which seems broken in water in Sixth Responses to show if there are any differences between his statements and Austin's about what is really perceived. I emphasize that considering that perception is a mental act does not implicate that the representations are the perceived objects. With the notion of « virtual reflexion » used by Arnauld in his Des vraies et des fausses idées, to explain the objective reality of ideas in Descartes' thought, I expose a cartesian philosophy of ideas which does not posit representative beings, and I examine under this light the radical austinian conception of perception.

1En rupture avec la tradition tant classique qu'analytique dans sa première mouture (Frege, Russell, Carnap), Austin a ouvert de multiples champs d'investigation, comme en témoignent le nombre, la diversité et l'extrême variété de ses continuateurs. Mais plus encore, grâce aux instruments conceptuels qu'il a polis, Austin permet de relire les grands textes de la philosophie classique. L'exemple le plus célèbre de cet « usage d'Austin » est la tentative d'Hintikka pour repenser le Cogito cartésien à la lumière du performatif. C'est cet « usage d'Austin » que l'A. voudrait ici déployer, et cela à partir de la tradition la plus apparemment éloignée et la plus violemment antithétique de celle qu'incarne Austin, à savoir l'idéalisme allemand. L'A. démontrera, tout d'abord, comment Fichte, auteur spéculatif par excellence, peut être comprís, philosophiquement et philologiquement, à partir des catégories les plus innovantes d'Austin. L'A. montrera ensuite comment, en retour, cette tradition spéculative, ainsi relue, permet d'interroger le champ ouvert par Austin. Peut-on, tel le déchiffreur d'anamorphoses, se placer à l'angle inhabituel de la pensée d'Austin pour voir le dessein formé par la pensée de Fichte et peut-on, en retour, se placer à l'angle inhabituel de la spéculation allemande pour découvrir un nouvel usage d'Austin ? Tel est le double pari de cet article qui étudiera d'abord Fichte sous l'angle d'Austin, puis Austin (et deux de ses continuateurs les plus opposés  Searle et Cavell) sous l'angle de la question nodale de l'idéalisme allemand. Par-delà l'opposition des traditions, l'A. fait le pari que cette sorte de fertilisation croisée des théories, loin de conduire à des êtres hybrides, peut produire un métissage fécond qui fit, de tout temps, la philosophie.

2That Austin broke with the classic tradition of philosophy as well as with the first analytical tradition of Frege, Russell and Carnap, and that he thus opened up many new fields to philosophical investigation, is clearly demonstrated by the number and diversity of his followers. But it is yet more noteworthy that the conceptual tools he so carefully wrought may be used to reinterpret the major works of classical philosophy. The most famous example of such a use is Hintikka's attempt to reinterpret Descartes' Cogito in performative terms. The present author intends to use Austin in a similar way in order to deal with the philosophical tradition that is apparently remotest from and most violently antithetical to Austin's own, i.e., German Idealism. Firstly, the author attempts to demonstrate how Fichte, the speculative philosopher par excellence, may be philosophically and philologically understood thanks to Austin's most innovative categories ; then, she shows how the speculative tradition, thus re-interpreted, may conversely help to re-examine the conceptual field Austin has opened up. Is it possible for today's reader of Fichte and Austin to straighten up this double anamorphosis ? May one see Fichte's design from the unusual viewpoint of Austin's thought, and conversely, by using the unusual viewpoint of German speculative philosophy may one discover unknown uses of Austin ? Such are the questions addressed in this article, which will deal first with Fichte from the viewpoint of Austin's theories, and then with Austin  along with two of his most radically opposed followers, Searle and Cavell  from the viewpoint of German Idealism. The present author believes that, beyond the conflict of traditions, such theoretical cross-fertilization, far from breeding hybrids, may on the contrary lead to this type of fecund métissage of which philosophy has always been made.
