1Following chromosome numbers (2n) have been found in Portulaca species: 8 in P. foliosa, 18 in P. grandiflora, 50 in P. quadrifida, 54 in P. oleracea, 108 in P. centrali-africana, P. kermesina and P. sp. P. foliosa and P. grandiflora had much larger chromosomes than the other species. Meiosis was very irregular in a hybrid between P. centrali-africana and P. kermesina; there were generally 10 to 13 bivalents. Polysomatic cells were frequent in adventitious roots, chiefly in P. foliosa and P. grandiflora. The presently studied species may be divided either in three groups characterized by three basic numbers (4, 9 and 25), or in two groups according to the chromosome length. In several groups, evolution went on by allopolyploidy.
