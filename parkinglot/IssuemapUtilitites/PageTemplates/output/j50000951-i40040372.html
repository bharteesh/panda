2This paper is grounded on Wittgenstein's thought to understand how economic rules operate. Starting from a study of a Workshop at the Paris Metro where a new productivity rule was introduced in 1993, we come to three conclusions. First, in the process of tasks selection the operatives do not apply rules in a mechanical way, nor do they interpret rules without reference to the uses. Our observations show that « obeying a rule is a practice ». Secondly, the teams develop different strategies to get the bonus. This illustrates another Wittgenstein's thesis : « The meaning of a rule appears in its use. » Finally, the operatives are based themselves on the rules that govern the existing uses to find a fair use of the rule.
