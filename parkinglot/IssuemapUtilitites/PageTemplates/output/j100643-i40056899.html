2This article breaks new ground by including a table of equivalents which shows how the string-lengths indicated by Wright translate into metronome values. Playing through the concerto at first hand the reader will find Wright's tempos to be more extreme in both directions than might have been expected. His extensive foreword to the concerto contains Wright's criticism of performers of his day who indulged in excessive speed and visually showy performance.
3Contemporary biographical accounts reveal Wright to have been innovative in other fields as well as music. In contrast to the declining interest shown by successive entries in Grove, he was in his day widely celebrated as an exceptionally gifted musician and an inspirational teacher.
