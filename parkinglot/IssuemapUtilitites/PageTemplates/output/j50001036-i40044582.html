2This article explores the musical practices of pre-teen girls in an Australian after school care centre. The girls' practices constituted "serious play" as they attempted to transform and reconfigure the space and challenge the power relations of the institutional setting both musically and sexually. After providing a snapshot of the girls' play, the article concludes that their musical engagement in after school care was related to constantly shifting fields of power and a struggle over western cultural beliefs regarding asexual childhood.

2"Bedroom culture" is an expression that encapsulates the new importance of the bedroom in the young generations' lives which has taken place during the past fifty years. A survey conducted near children aged 7 to 13 and their parents reveals the differences of meaning of this room, which is at first a space to play, then a place to develop one's autonomy and then expressivity, while being under parental control. This survey reveals the new precocity of children which is linked to the development of the new multimedia technologies, if not permitted by them.

2Sharing daily family time with adolescents is always marked by tensions. On the one hand, these moments are controlled by parental rules governing life in common and teenagers have to keep to their set place within a family hierarchy. On the other hand, shared moments can also be times when the teenager's individuality is acknowledged, building family togetherness. In this case, the teenager can partake of a relation based on a more egalitarian relationship. The duality of these moments in families with teenagers is best understood through the concept of being "free together".
3In der Phase des Erwachsenwerdens ist der Familienalltag von Spannungen geprägt.
