1This article is a proposal for creating a foreign policy position with moral dimensions. However, this manuscript goes beyond this concern to the basic qualities that must be considered in the war of ideas with our adversaries.
2These qualities include: the political characteristics we wish to expose to the world, the adoption of positions consistent with our democratic heritage, and the education programs that offer a defense of Western principles. In these three areas there are arguable weaknesses. Our efforts to inform the international community of the US national purpose have been deterred by a lack of confidence in our tradition and hesitancy in representing it to the world. Our view of democracy has been vitiated by relativism, i.e., "this system is only one of many but is neither better nor worse than the others." Our educational system has undermined belief in the national purpose through such programs as nuclear education, which are ostensibly propagandistic efforts for unilateral nuclear disarmament. If we are to prevail in this global community fraught with schisms, we must reassert our moral posture, regain a sense of national purpose, and provide our young with a belief in this system. This paper is an effort to achieve these goals.
