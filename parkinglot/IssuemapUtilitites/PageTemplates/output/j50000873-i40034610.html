1I. La capanna: 1. Introduzione; 2. Rituale di construzione: la libagione; 3. Materiale da construzione; 4. Tipi di abitazione; 5. Cucina e corredo domestico; 6. Elementi complementari nel casale; - II. Il gruppo domestico: 1. Fattori di mobilità nella distribuzione degli individui; 2. Problemi di distribuzione nella famiglia poliginica; 3. Famiglia e gruppo domestico, capanna e casale; 4. Composizione e scomposizione di gruppi unità; 5. La fissione ciclica.
2This article deals with house-building pattern and domestic group among the Nzema of Ghana. Technical solutions in building according to the different habitats and consequently available building materials are considered; the article thus concentrates in three different building standards: the wooden one on the coastal zone, the mud standard in the interior and the pile hut in lagoon villages.
3Secondly the autor proceeds studying house and compound population with respect to distribution and individual mobility within the hut and the huts lot forming a compound, according to sex and age. Then the birth of unifunctional groups such as the sleeping units of people, the earning units, the cooking units and the eating units is examined.
4Birth, cleavage and recomposition of such groups by its internal replacement in personnel have been studied on the basis of J. Goody principles as they are stated in some of his essays.
5L'article traite de l'habitation et du groupe domestique chez les Nzema du Ghana. Dans la première partie sont examinées les solutions techniques adoptées dans la construction des huttes qui se distinguent dans les diverses zones géographiques et selon le materiel de construction disponible. Trois types fondamentaux de huttes sont individúes: les huttes de branches de la côte, les huttes de terre battue de la zone intérieure et les huttes de pilotis sur les lagunes.
6Dans la seconde partie sont observées la distribution et la mobilité des individus à l'intérieur des huttes et de l'ensemble des huttes formant un compound et enfin le dynamisme de formation des groupes monofonctionnels tels que les groupes des personnes qui cohabitent, qui travaillent, qui cuisinent et qui mangent ensemble.
7La formation, la dispersion et la recomposition de ces groupes à travers le changement des composants ont été analysés sur la base des principes exposés par J. Goody dans certains de ses articles.
